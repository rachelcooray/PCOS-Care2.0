About PCOS

What is PCOS?

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder that affects women of reproductive age. It can lead to irregular periods, excessive hair growth, acne, weight gain, and fertility issues. PCOS is also linked to insulin resistance, increasing the risk of diabetes, heart disease, and other metabolic disorders.

Common Symptoms

  • Irregular periods or no periods at all
  • Difficulty getting pregnant due to irregular ovulation
  • Excessive hair growth (hirsutism) - usually on face, chest, back
  • Weight gain
  • Thinning hair and hair loss from the head
  • Oily skin or acne

Why Early Detection Matters

Early detection and lifestyle changes can help manage symptoms and prevent long-term complications.

Without treatment, PCOS can increase the risk of:

  • Type 2 Diabetes: Due to insulin resistance.
  • Cardiovascular Disease: Increased risk of high cholesterol and hypertension.
  • Infertility & Pregnancy Complications: Affecting ovulation and hormone balance.
  • Mental Health Challenges: Anxiety and depression are common among those with PCOS.

How PCOS Affects Health

PCOS affects multiple systems in the body, leading to:

  • Hormonal Imbalances: Excess androgen levels leading to acne and hair growth.
  • Metabolic Issues: Increased insulin resistance, causing weight gain and high blood sugar.
  • Fertility Challenges: Irregular ovulation affecting pregnancy.
  • Increased Risk of Chronic Diseases: Heart disease, diabetes, and depression.

Based on information from: WHO

PCOS Management Tips

Managing PCOS involves lifestyle modifications, medical treatments, and self-care. Here are some key tips:

  • Regular Exercise: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week.
  • Balanced Nutrition: Focus on whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats.
  • Stress Management: Practice yoga, meditation, or deep breathing.
  • Quality Sleep: Ensure 7-9 hours of sleep per night.
  • Medical Support: Consult a doctor for medications and hormone management if needed.

Based on information from: British Dietetic Association (BDA)

Dietary Recommendations for PCOS

  • Foods to Include:
    • Whole grains: Quinoa, brown rice, etc.
    • Healthy fats: Avocado, olive oil, nuts.
    • Lean proteins: Chicken, tofu, fish, legumes.
    • Fiber-rich vegetables: Broccoli, spinach, non-starchy veg.

  • Foods to Avoid:
    • Processed foods and sugary snacks.
    • Excessive dairy and refined carbohydrates.
    • Fried and fast foods.

  • Hydration: Drink plenty of water.
  • Meal Timing: Balanced meals with controlled portions.

Based on information from: Johns Hopkins Medicine

Insight 1: The Growing Challenge - PCOS Prevalence

PCOS affects millions of people around the world, but many don’t even know they have it. This is because PCOS is often not diagnosed, and many people overlook the symptoms. Understanding how common PCOS is is the first step toward managing it better. This section highlights an alarming number of people with undiagnosed PCOS, which is a major issue.

Insight 3: The Faces of PCOS - Common Symptoms Breakdown

PCOS can cause a variety of symptoms that affect a person’s daily life. Some of the most common symptoms include pimples, weight gain, skin darkening, hair loss, and extra hair growth. It’s important to recognize these signs, as they can help people seek the right treatment. Lifestyle changes can help manage these symptoms and improve overall well-being.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How do I use the PCOS Risk Assessment Tool?

Simply fill in your details in the input fields on the Risk Assessment page and click submit. The results will be displayed on the Results page.

Can I get a detailed report of my risk assessment?

Yes! You can receive a full report with a summary and prediction, and you can download it as a PDF for your reference.

What happens if I don't fill out all the fields?

All fields are required for an accurate risk assessment. Make sure you complete everything to get the most accurate results.

Is my data safe?

Yes, privacy is taken seriously. Your data is not saved after the session, and it is only used for the assessment.

Can I use this tool for someone else?

This tool is designed for personal use, but you can enter details for someone else as long as you have their consent.

Is this a medical diagnosis?

No, this tool is intended for preliminary assessment only and should not be used as a substitute for professional medical advice.

What is the accuracy of the prediction?

The accuracy is based on data from 541 patients in Kerala, India, so it may not be 100% accurate for every person.

Does this tool integrate with healthcare systems?

No, this is a standalone tool and currently does not link with any medical records or healthcare systems.
